## Purpose

Define the first MVP adventure premise for AIGMOS at a scope and product validation level.

This document does not define the full adventure, final dialogue, complete stat blocks, or a complete world setting.

## Adventure One-Sentence Pitch

Working title: The Candlewick Mine Incident

A lone adventurer arrives in a storm-battered village where miners have disappeared, strange lights flicker inside an abandoned mine, and the truth depends on what the player chooses to investigate, trust, or confront.

## Player Fantasy

The player should feel like a lone adventurer being personally guided by a capable AI Game Master through a focused but responsive TRPG session.

The experience should emphasize:

- Personal agency within a constrained mystery.
- Clear scene framing from the AI Game Master.
- Meaningful choices about investigation, trust, and confrontation.
- A sense that NPCs remember what the player has asked, revealed, or promised.
- A clean adventure ending within one short session.

## Scope

The first adventure is a 30-60 minute solo fantasy mystery adventure set in a small rain-soaked frontier village and a nearby abandoned mine.

MVP scope includes:

- One village
- One abandoned mine
- One central danger
- Three major NPC roles
- One investigation phase
- One mine exploration phase
- One danger/combat/confrontation phase
- Three possible ending categories

## Core Locations

The adventure uses a small set of locations so the AI Game Master can maintain coherence during the MVP session.

Core locations:

- Village inn
- Village square
- Mine entrance
- Mine interior
- Final chamber

Location purposes:

- Village inn: First social anchor and possible source of rumors or withheld information.
- Village square: Public pressure point where the village's fear and practical needs are visible.
- Mine entrance: Threshold between investigation and exploration.
- Mine interior: Constrained exploration space containing clues, danger, and rising tension.
- Final chamber: Resolution space for revelation, confrontation, rescue, or final choice.

## Core NPC Roles

The MVP adventure includes three major NPC roles.

Core NPC roles:

- Innkeeper who knows more than they first reveal.
- Village leader who wants the problem solved quietly.
- Relative of a missing miner who pushes for urgency.

NPC design intent:

- Each NPC should provide a different angle on the disappearances.
- Each NPC should have a reason to shape, withhold, or emphasize information.
- NPC memory should matter within the session, especially if the player confronts contradictions or returns with new evidence.

## Core Conflict

The village wants the disappearances resolved, but different NPCs are hiding different parts of the truth. The mine contains a danger that should be understandable within one short session.

The conflict should support mystery without requiring a large lore explanation or a complete world setting.

The central danger should be:

- Simple enough to understand during one short session.
- Flexible enough to support rescue, confrontation, or truth-revealing endings.
- Constrained enough that the AI Game Master can track its state reliably.

## Expected Gameplay Beats

The expected session flow includes:

- Arrival in storm
- First briefing
- Player chooses who to question or where to inspect
- First clue
- Entry into mine
- Environmental danger or small hostile encounter
- Revelation
- Final choice or confrontation
- Ending summary

These beats are not final scene scripts. They define the intended adventure rhythm for the MVP.

## MVP Validation Goals

This adventure should help validate whether AIGMOS can support a small, coherent solo TRPG session.

Validation goals:

- Test if AI GM can keep a small mystery coherent.
- Test if player free-text choices can affect scene order.
- Test if NPC memory matters within one session.
- Test if one short adventure can end cleanly.
- Test if save/resume can preserve mystery state.

## Constraints

The first adventure must stay within MVP limits.

Constraints:

- Solo play only.
- Text-only presentation.
- No open world expansion.
- No full campaign continuity beyond session save/resume.
- No full D&D ruleset requirement.
- No full class, spell, or monster system.
- No final dialogue in this document.
- No complete stat blocks in this document.
- No complete world setting in this document.

The AI Game Master should not imply that excluded MVP features are available.
